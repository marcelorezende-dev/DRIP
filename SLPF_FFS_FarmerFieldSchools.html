DRIP Knowledge Library · SLPF Pillar 03 – Farmer Field Schools

Learning in the field

Farmer Field Schools are the experiential, farmer-to-farmer engine of the SLPF – participatory learning on demonstration plots that transfers sustainable land and forest management from practice to practice.

FFS provide a participatory, experiential platform for technology transfer and sustainable land management (FAO, 2016). Through peer-to-peer exchange, hands-on training and skill development, farmers test and adopt SLM/SFM practices on shared demonstration plots – knowledge co-created in the field rather than delivered from a podium (Waddington & White, 2014; Bezner Kerr et al., 2021).

What it delivers

Proven practicesTransfers proven and innovative SLM/SFM practices through context-sensitive, participatory methods.
ExperientialHands-on, season-long learning on demonstration plots – farmers see results before they adopt.
Farmer-to-farmer cascadeTrained farmers become the next cohort's teachers, embedding capacity in the community.

Results by mid-term

14,000+ha community forest management
2,249ha assisted natural regeneration
39%reduction in veld-fire incidence

Where it sits in the framework

Assessment → sustainability

FFS runs from a farmer-knowledge baseline and behaviour-change diagnosis, through curriculum design and site selection, to seasonal FFS cycles on demonstration plots – tracked by practice-adoption rates and yield comparisons, and sustained through the farmer-to-farmer cascade and institutional embedding.

It activates the other pillars

FFS is where Community Seed Bank germplasm is trialled and Forest and Farm Facility practices are learned. Its baseline draws on the ILAM household and behaviour-change module (SHARP+ and COM-B).
