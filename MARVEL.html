What MARVEL is

MARVEL joins the two faces of results work: MRV – Monitoring, Reporting and Verification of environmental results – and MEL – Monitoring, Evaluation and Learning that feeds lessons back into delivery. Together they turn the Programme's activity into evidence, accountability and improvement.

M
Monitoring

Track results from the community up to GEF reporting.

A
Assessment

Establish the baseline and measure change (ILAM).

R
Reporting

PIRs and core-indicator reporting against targets.

V
Verification

Validate data quality and the environmental benefits claimed.

E
Evaluation

Judge what worked, where and why.

L
Learning

Feed lessons back through the Regional Exchange Mechanism.

ProjectsEleven child projects, one programme

The DSL-IP delivers through eleven country (child) projects coordinated by a Global Coordination Project. They span the dryland forests and rangelands of southern and eastern Africa, the Sahel and the Central Asian steppe.

A biome in focus

Most of the southern-African projects sit in the miombo & mopane woodlands – the African companion to Brazil's Caatinga, and the heartland of the dryland-forests agenda.

Indicators and GEBsGlobal Environmental Benefits

The Programme reports against the GEF-7 Core Indicators. Its headline global environmental benefit targets, across the eleven countries, are:

Climate change mitigation

46.3M tonnes CO₂e of greenhouse-gas emissions mitigated (GEF-7 CI-6).

Land restored and managed

13.1M hectares of land restored or brought under improved management (GEF-7 CI-3 / CI-4).

Reach

11 countries, with results flowing from communities up to GEF reporting through the shared monitoring system.

How the numbers are assured

Targets are tracked through the participatory Monitoring System (the MRV engine), baselined with ILAM, and reported in annual PIRs – the Verification step before benefits are claimed.
