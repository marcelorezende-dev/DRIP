Water Harvesting · Micro-catchment

Trapezoidal bunds

Trapezoid bunds that pond runoff over a block of field crops

What it is

Trapezoidal bunds are trapezoid-shaped earth bunds – a base bund built across the slope with two wing walls angled upstream – that pond harvested runoff over a larger cropped area than semi-circular bunds. The enclosed block fills with water that soaks in to support the crop, while excess overflows safely around the wing tips (Critchley & Siegert, 1991).

They are used for field crops in larger micro- and macro-catchments, and in the WOCAT classification belong to the micro-catchment water-harvesting group, where a bare catchment feeds an adjacent cropped area.

How it works

  • Build a base bund across the slope with two wing walls angled upstream
  • Runoff ponds in the enclosed area, and excess overflows round the wing tips
  • Crop the enclosed block once the harvested water has wetted the soil
  • Set the bunds out in a staggered series down the slope so each catches what passes the units above
