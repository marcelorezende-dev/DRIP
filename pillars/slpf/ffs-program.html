Peer-Farmer Learning · Programme

Farmer Field School programme

A participatory, experiential platform for technology transfer and sustainable land management. Farmers meet through a cropping season on a shared demonstration plot, observe and compare, and carry what works back to their own fields – and to the next farmer.

What the programme is

A field is the classroom; the season is the syllabus; the farmers are the teachers.

Farmer Field Schools (FFS) provide a participatory, experiential platform for technology transfer and sustainable land management (FAO, 2016). Through peer-to-peer exchange, hands-on training and skill development, farmers gain practical techniques to raise productivity while conserving landscape integrity – strengthening community cohesion and adaptive capacity along the way (Waddington & White, 2014).

What the programme delivers

  • Transfers proven and innovative SLM/SFM practices through context-sensitive, participatory methods.
  • Builds farmer capacity to diagnose, analyse and respond to land degradation – linking technical training to landscape objectives.
  • Embeds a robust exit strategy so adoption endures – knowledge is co-created, not imposed (Bezner Kerr et al., 2021).

How it joins the other streams

  • Runs the seasonal FFS cycles and demonstration plots that put SLPF practices to the test.
  • Draws germplasm from Community Seed Banks and feeds produce into green value chains.
  • Sequenced for each landscape through the ICDIP – curriculum design and FFS site selection.
