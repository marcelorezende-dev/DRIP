Recaatingamento

Community-led conservation and recomposition of the Caatinga (IRPAA)

What it is

Recaatingamento is a community approach led by IRPAA (Instituto Regional da Pequena Agropecuária Apropriada) with small fundo-de-pasto producers in Bahia, engaging them directly in caring for the Caatinga. It runs on five lines of action and is beginning to add sustainable pastoral use that cancels the impact of over-grazing by adjusting stocking rates (Araújo Filho, 2013).

How it works

  • 1 · Conservation of the Caatinga
  • 2 · Recomposition – nurseries and transplanting of native species
  • 3 · Contextualised environmental education
  • 4 · Income – value-added agro-extractivism (umbu, wild passion-fruit)
  • 5 · Public policies
  • Sustainable pastoral use with stocking rates set to remove the over-grazing impact (Araújo Filho, 2013)

Key facts

Lead

Note

An approach rather than a single technique – it pairs ecological recomposition with income, education and rights so the practice sticks.
