Caroá

Sustainable harvest of caroá fibre – cordage, crafts and textiles

What it is

Sustainable harvest of caroá (Neoglaziovia variegata), a native bromeliad whose leaves yield a strong fibre used for cordage, crafts and textiles – a classic non-timber forest product of the Caatinga, documented in the MAPA good-practice guide (MAPA, 2015). Mature leaves are cut selectively, leaving plants and a share of leaves for regeneration (MAPA, 2015).

How it works

  • Cut mature leaves selectively, by hand, in season
  • Leave plants and a share of leaves for regeneration
  • Ret and process the fibre; clean, organic handling
  • Add value and market through a group

Key facts

Product
caroá fibre (cordage, crafts, textiles)
Plant
native bromeliad
Where
across the semi-arid (PE, BA)
Status
organic extractivism (MAPA good practice)
Note

A low-input fibre value chain that rewards keeping the native stand in place.
