Section 2–3 · The roadmap

Three action pillars, applied bottom-up

Stakeholders at the global gender workshop defined three pillars that form the roadmap for the DSL-IP Gender Community of Practice (CoP3). They are implemented stepwise – from country-informed technical assistance, through monitoring and evaluation, to knowledge management and outreach – so that gender mainstreaming is a long-term programme commitment, not a one-off, in line with FAO's corporate gender framework (FAO, 2020).

1

Technical Assistance

Pillar 1 · TA

Gender-specific guidance to child projects – refining country Gender Action Plans and backstopping experts to integrate gender-responsive actions into mainstream project activities, building on ILAM evidence and the core-theme approach.

2

Monitoring & Evaluation

Pillar 2 · M&E

Analysing gender inequalities and tracking results across scales. Sex-disaggregated indicators flow into the DSL-IP M&E system and country results frameworks, reported through PIR, Mid-Term and Terminal Evaluations.

3

Knowledge Management & Outreach

Pillar 3 · KMO

Documenting and exchanging promising gender-responsive practices from the ground – case studies, human-interest stories and knowledge products – and amplifying women leaders across DSL-IP and partner channels.

Section 7 · Where results are generated

Six Project Core Areas

Component 2 – linked to the Sustainable Landscape Production Framework – is the priority for gender results. Six Project Core Areas (PCAs) focus the refinement of gender plans, the tracking of core indicators, and the reporting of the most relevant results at country level.

Closing three overarching gender gaps

By building the gender advantage through the PCAs, the action pillars contribute to addressing the three gender gaps of priority to the GEF (Global Environment Facility, 2017).

Gap 01

Unequal access to and control of natural resources

Land, water and other productive assets and biological resources.

Gap 02

Unbalanced participation and leadership in decision-making

Institutions and policymaking, environmental planning, and sustainable management of natural resources.

Gap 03

Uneven access to socio-economic benefits and services

Income generation, credit, technology, financing, training and information.
