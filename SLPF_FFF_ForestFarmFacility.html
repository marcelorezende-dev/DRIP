DRIP Knowledge Library · SLPF Pillar 02 – Forest and Farm Facility

Making sustainability pay

The Forest and Farm Facility incubates producer enterprises along green value chains – so that sustainable landscape production rewards farmers better than the activities that degrade the land.

The FFF pillar focuses on business incubation and growth for forest and farm producer organizations (FFPOs) in green value chains – market-oriented systems that add value to forest and farm products while safeguarding the environment (FAO & AgriCord, 2016). It answers the question every land user asks: does conserving the landscape pay better than degrading it?

What it does

Captures more valueFrom NTFPs, sustainable charcoal, bee products, ecotourism and drought-tolerant crops – creating alternatives to environmentally harmful activities.
Incubates enterpriseBusiness-plan development, market linkages and access to finance for FFPOs, so groups can grow viable green enterprises.
Rewards stewardshipAligns income with conservation, so the market itself pulls land users towards sustainable landscape production. Value-chain analysis shows where producers' bargaining power and value capture can be strengthened (Kaplinsky, 2000).

Where it sits in the framework

Assessment → sustainability

FFF moves from value-chain assessment and FFPO capacity review, to business-plan development and market-linkage strategy, to green-value-chain development and enterprise incubation, tracked through income and market-access indicators, with FFPO strengthening as the exit strategy.

It monetizes the other pillars

FFF turns the germplasm of the Community Seed Banks and the practices taught in Farmer Field Schools into income – the economic incentive that makes adoption stick. Its entry points come from the ILAM value-chain assessment.
