Baobab & Marula Value Chains

Zimbabwe’s core theme — high-value products from iconic standing trees

What it is

Zimbabwe’s championed theme builds value chains around two iconic standing trees — baobab (Adansonia digitata) and marula (Sclerocarya birrea). Their fruit, oil and kernels are processed into high-value, certifiable products, rewarding communities for keeping the big trees in the landscape rather than clearing them.

How it works

  • Harvest fruit, seeds and kernels from naturally standing baobab and marula — no felling
  • Process into pulp, oil, juice and powder for food, cosmetics and export markets
  • Meet organic / fair-trade certification to capture premium prices
  • Protect mature and regenerating trees as the productive asset of the value chain
  • Aggregate and add value through FFPOs for quality, volume and market reach

Why it matters

These are non-wood forest products from trees that take decades to grow. A working value chain makes each standing tree an annual income — the clearest incentive there is to keep it (Dewees et al., 2010).
