Capacity & exchangeThe FAO Drylands School

The FAO Drylands School is a global platform for training, capacity development and the exchange of best practices in drylands. Established in 2022, it trains “champions” – the planners, practitioners and policymakers who advocate for and operationalize transformative change in the management of dryland forests, rangelands and agrosilvopastoral systems.

The School is an initiative of the Committee on Forestry Working Group on Dryland Forests and Agrosilvopastoral Systems (the COFO WG, a subsidiary body of FAO's Committee on Forestry established in 2016). Each edition is run in partnership with universities, research institutes, UN bodies and host governments, and is held back-to-back with a session of the COFO Working Group. The School also supports the UN International Year of Rangelands and Pastoralists (IYRP) 2026 and connects to the Drylands Sustainable Landscapes Impact Programme (DSL-IP).

It is a professionally facilitated, four-day course delivered in English and built on experiential learning: participants bring and critically reflect on their own case studies, learn from technical lectures and peers, and take part in a field day. Champions are selected from an open call – 20 to 25 per cohort – with deliberate attention to gender, geographic and other diversity. On completion they receive a certificate and a digital badge, and join a standing, South–South alumni network that keeps exchanging knowledge between editions.

What it teaches

The curriculum follows the COFO WG's transformational approach to building climate-resilient drylands. Across the editions it has covered the assessment of dryland actions and cross-cutting indicators (2023), participatory monitoring of restoration in agrosilvopastoral systems (2024), the inclusive and equitable governance of dryland forests, rangelands and pastoral systems (2025), and Indigenous knowledge and fire management (2026). Foundations are built on the FAO eLearning Academy course Transforming dryland forests and agrosilvopastoral systems, which participants complete before they arrive. Read the two-page brief →

The approachHow the School works

Every edition follows the same arc – from an online foundation to a standing network of champions.

01
eLearning foundation

Selected participants first complete the FAO eLearning course on transforming dryland forests and agrosilvopastoral systems, and prepare a case study from their own work.

02
Open call & selection

Partners disseminate an open call; 20–25 champions are selected on motivation, capacity and potential impact, balancing gender, geography and diversity.

03
The four-day school

A facilitated, English-language course of lectures, group work on participants' own case studies and peer-to-peer learning, hosted in a dryland country.

04
Field day

A day in the field with local communities and stakeholders – seeing restoration, rangeland and agrosilvopastoral practice first-hand.

05
Certificate & network

Graduates receive a certificate and digital badge and join the alumni network – a growing community of practice with continued post-school engagement.

EditionsFour schools, four drylands

Each edition is hosted in a different dryland region and built around a theme, back-to-back with a session of the COFO Working Group.

20231st edition

Amman, Jordan

September 2023 · 4 days · alongside the COFO WG 3rd session

Transformative approaches in agrosilvopastoral systems. The inaugural school brought dryland planners and practitioners together to assess their current and planned actions and to define cross-cutting indicators of progress.

Partners: Agricultural Research Council of South Africa · Jordan University of Science and Technology · Sokoine University of Agriculture (Tanzania) · Swedish University of Agricultural Sciences · University of Jordan · WOCAT
20242nd edition

Nairobi, Kenya

12–15 September 2024 · CIFOR-ICRAF campus · field trip 14 Sep

Monitoring restoration in agrosilvopastoral systems for sustainability and ecosystem services. A cohort focused on participatory monitoring – adapting frameworks, co-defining indicators with local stakeholders and gathering field data, with a special focus on pastoralism.

Partners: UNCCD G20 Global Land Initiative (lead sponsor) · Community Jameel · CIFOR-ICRAF · WOCAT / CDE University of Bern · SLU · Jameel Observatory · ARC-LNR South Africa · IYRP 2026 · DSL-IP · Kenya Forest Service
20253rd edition

Ulaanbaatar, Mongolia

7–10 July 2025 · alongside the COFO WG 4th session

Improving the governance of dryland forests, rangelands and agrosilvopastoral systems. Hosted by the Government of Mongolia, the school addressed inclusive, gender-responsive and equitable land governance, in direct support of the IYRP 2026.

Partners: Government of Mongolia · IYRP Global Alliance · COFO WG partners
20264th edition

Adelaide, South Australia Upcoming

July 2026 · hosted by Adelaide University

Indigenous knowledges and practices in drylands and fire management. The next edition focuses on integrating Indigenous knowledge and participatory fire management into climate-resilient dryland governance.

Partners: Adelaide University · Government of South Australia

Get involvedJoin a future cohort

The FAO Drylands School recruits its champions through an open call disseminated by FAO and the School's partners ahead of each edition.

Take part

To be ready for the next call, complete the FAO eLearning Academy course Transforming dryland forests and agrosilvopastoral systems and prepare a short case study from your own work in drylands. Calls and selection are coordinated by the COFO Working Group on Dryland Forests and Agrosilvopastoral Systems secretariat, together with the host government and partners for each edition.

Alumni are encouraged to stay engaged – mentoring new champions, sharing field experience and contributing to the network between editions.
