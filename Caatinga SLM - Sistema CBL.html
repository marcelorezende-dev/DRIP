SLM Technology · Silvopastoral

Sistema CBL

Caatinga · Buffel grass · Leucaena – a three-part silvopastoral year

What it is

The CBL system, developed at Embrapa Semiárido (Petrolina), combines native Caatinga with a buffel-grass pasture and a protein bank of leucaena (or another forage legume) for hay/silage and browse. The tree-Caatinga makes up one-third to two-thirds of the area and carries the herd through the rains; the other components carry it through the dry season (Araújo Filho, 2013).

How it works

  • Graze the tree-Caatinga in the rainy months (2–4 months) (Araújo Filho, 2013)
  • Move to the buffel-grass pasture, with the leucaena protein bank in the early dry season
  • Feed on hay produced during the rains through the rest of the dry season (Araújo Filho, 2013)

Key facts

Components
Caatinga + buffel + leucaena
Caatinga share
1/3 – 2/3 of area
Origin
Embrapa Semiárido, Petrolina (PE)
Best for
goats, sheep, cattle
Note

A production system that sequences three feed sources so the herd is fed year-round without clearing the native forest.
