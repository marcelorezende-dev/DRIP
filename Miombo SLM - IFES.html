IFES — Integrated Energy & Food Systems

Malawi’s core theme — closing the loop between food, soil and household energy on one plot

What it is

Integrated Energy and Food Systems (IFES) is Malawi’s championed core theme under the DSL-IP — an approach that produces food, soil fertility and household energy together on the same plot, so a family meets its energy needs without drawing ever more wood from the woodland. It is delivered through the Sisteminha–IFES model and the SLPF pillars.

How it works

  • Intercrop a nitrogen-fixing legume (pigeon pea) with the staple cereal (maize) — food, fodder and fertility from one field
  • Feed the legume’s biomass and residues to soil and livestock, lifting the next season’s yield
  • Adopt fuel-efficient stoves and on-farm biogas to cut the woodfuel a household draws from the woodland
  • Recycle crop and animal residues into energy and compost, closing the food–soil–energy loop
  • Deliver farmer-to-farmer through Farmer Field Schools and producer organisations so adoption endures

Why it matters

Most rural households in the miombo cook with firewood and charcoal drawn from the woodland (Ryan et al., 2016; FAO, 2017). By growing food and producing energy on the same plot, IFES eases that pressure at its source — making the standing woodland worth more than the fuel it could be cut for.
