SAF-Sobral

An integrated crop–livestock–forest layout from 3 hectares up

What it is

SAF-Sobral is Embrapa Caprinos’ successful agrosilvopastoral model, integrating cropping, livestock and the strategic use of the Caatinga. The holding is divided into three parcels and works from about three hectares up (Araújo Filho, 2013).

How it works

  • Crop parcel (20 percent): alley cropping – grain and hay in the rains, a protein bank in the dry season
  • Pastoral parcel (60 percent): thinned or enriched Caatinga grazed year-round by cattle, sheep or goats
  • Legal reserve (20 percent): managed as a woodlot or an apiary (Araújo Filho, 2013)

Key facts

Layout
20% crops · 60% pasture · 20% reserve
Maize yield
79% above the state average
Yield variability
CV 19.8% vs 75.5% statewide
Land use
same plot each year – no shifting cultivation
Origin
Embrapa Caprinos e Ovinos, Sobral (CE)
Note

The flagship whole-farm system – higher and far steadier production on a fixed plot, with a quarter of the land kept as reserve (Araújo Filho, 2013).
