Rangeland & Agro-sylvo-pastoral Management

Working the woodland with livestock without grazing it bare

What it is

Across the drier woodland, livestock graze and browse the standing trees year-round. Managed well — with rest, rotation and resilient seed — this agro-sylvo-pastoral system keeps the grass-and-browse layer recovering instead of grazing it bare. It is shared with the Programme’s drylands cross-learning groups (Kenya, Burkina Faso).

How it works

  • Rotate and rest paddocks so the grass-and-browse layer recovers between grazings
  • Use the standing woodland — mopane leaves and pods above all — as dry-season browse
  • Match stocking rates to the single, variable wet season to avoid overgrazing
  • Adopt drought-tolerant pasture varieties and community seed security
  • Integrate trees, grazing and crops in one agro-sylvo-pastoral system

Why it matters

The miombo–mopane is grazed year-round; the difference between use and ruin is rest and timing (Campbell, 1996). Rotational, woodland-integrated grazing lets the system carry livestock and recover — keeping both the pasture and the trees.
